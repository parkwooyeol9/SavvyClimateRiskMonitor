      Methodology (demo v1)

      How scores are produced today

      Transparent enough to explain in a meeting — and clearly labeled as demo, not scientific CMIP6 output.

      1. Asset baseline

      Each demo site has a base score (0–100) per hazard — flood, coastal, heat, drought, wildfire, wind, sea-level rise, water stress — tuned by region and sector.

      2. Scenario multipliers

      SSP2-4.5 and SSP5-8.5 apply horizon multipliers for 2030 / 2040 / 2050. Heat and flood get small extra boosts at worse pathways / later years.

      3. Composite & flags

      Composite = average of hazard scores. Elevated = composite ≥ 60. Top driver = highest average hazard across the filtered portfolio.

      4. Transition layer

      Carbon price, stranded %, and disorderly VaR follow a simple NGFS-style table keyed by scenario and scaled by horizon — directional, not a full IAM.

      What is real vs. illustrative

      • Real patterns: IPCC SSP framing, TCFD/ISSB topics, NGFS-style transition language, Leaflet/OpenStreetMap geography.
      • Illustrative data: hazard scores, VaR dollars, alert copy — engineered for demos, not underwriting or disclosure filings.
      • Next data layer: open climate APIs (e.g. Open-Meteo, NASA POWER, Copernicus CMIP6) + documented vulnerability by occupancy type.

      FAQ

      Common questions

      Is this live climate science data?

      Not yet. Scores are a transparent demo engine in the browser. Geography uses real map tiles; risk numbers are illustrative until open climate APIs are wired in.

      Can I upload my own portfolio?

      CSV export of the demo book works today. Portfolio upload and saved workspaces are on the roadmap after auth + a small backend.

      What does a score of 70+ mean?

      In this demo, composites ≥ 70 are flagged "high," 45–69 "medium," below 45 "low." Thresholds are for exploration — not insurance rating standards.

      SSP2-4.5 vs SSP5-8.5 — which should I use?

      SSP2-4.5 is a middle-of-the-road pathway; SSP5-8.5 is a high-emissions stress. Compare both in the dashboard — boards often want the gap, not a single number.

      Are the Europe death numbers counted live on this site?

      No. EuroMOMO and research estimates arrive with lag and revisions. We show a dated snapshot (as of mid-July 2026 reporting) plus live weather for situational awareness. Official heat-death tallies from agencies can take weeks.

      Where do the live market numbers come from?

      Server-side Vercel functions call FRED (energy prices + history), Finnhub (ETF quotes, candles, headlines), and FMP as fallback — reusing API keys from the SavvyETF project. Keys never ship to the browser; charts only receive JSON.

      Why not put the API key in the webpage JavaScript?

      Anyone can View Source and steal it, then burn your quota. Finance sites solve this the same way we do: a private backend key + a public chart endpoint.

      Is SavvyClimateRiskMonitor.com available?

      The product currently runs on a free Vercel URL (*.vercel.app). A custom .com is optional: buy the domain, then attach it in Vercel Domains — same site, your brand on the URL.

      Is this financial or insurance advice?

      No. Demo content only. Do not use it for underwriting, investment decisions, or regulatory filings without independent validated data and advice.
